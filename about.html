I am currently a second-year Environmental Engineering Student at the University of Texas at Austin! I am passionate about using engineering to create sustainable solutions for various problems. Within Environmental Engineering, I'm interested in sustainable technology, water treatment, consulting, and compliance.

In fifth grade, my teacher showed our class Annie Leonard’s documentary, the Story of Bottled Water. Ever since that pivotal moment, I decided that I would continuously learn about how I can help the environment and reduce my negative impact. I also discovered that I am immensely interested in subjects with science, math, engineering, and technology (STEM). I wondered if there was a way to combine my love of the environment with my interest in STEM.

After looking into my options, I decided to pursue the field of environmental engineering, which would combine two of my greatest passions. I wanted to learn more about the environmental engineering field, so I applied to be a part of the Independent Study and Mentorship program in my high school. As a part of this program, I was able to connect with a professional Environmental Engineer who mentored me for a semester. With her guidance, I learned so much about the software used, the professional communication skills required, and the types of projects done by environmental engineers.

I would like to continue to explore the field of environmental engineering and see how I can make a positive impact in my community. I hope to learn, grow, and make the world a better place.
